## 2. UK GUIDELINES (NICE NG73 — Updated 2024)

### Primary Guideline: NICE NG73 "Endometriosis: Diagnosis and Management"
- **Originally published:** September 2017
- **Updated:** November 2024 (diagnosis section); April 2024 (surgical management when fertility is a priority)
- **URL:** https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng73

### Key Recommendations Relevant to Your Application:

#### A. Managed Clinical Network (MCN) Structure (Recommendation 1.1.1)
NICE recommends setting up a **managed clinical network** consisting of:
- **Community services:** GPs, practice nurses, school nurses, sexual health services
- **Gynaecology services** (general)
- **Specialist endometriosis services** (BSGE-accredited endometriosis centres)

This **directly maps** to your triage categories:
| Your Triage Group | NICE MCN Level |
|---|---|
| Medical management | Community / General Gynaecology |
| Surgery — general gynaecologist pool | Gynaecology services |
| Surgery — specialist endometriosis surgeon | Specialist endometriosis centres (BSGE centres) |

#### B. Referral Criteria (Updated 2024)
- **Refer to specialist endometriosis centre** if suspected/confirmed **deep endometriosis** involving bowel, bladder, or ureter
- **Refer to gynaecology services** for suspected endometriosis not responding to medical management
- **Normal imaging should NOT exclude diagnosis** or delay management
- Transvaginal ultrasound and pelvic MRI are key for deep endometriosis assessment
- **Referral should not be delayed** if clinical suspicion is high even with negative imaging

#### C. NICE Quality Standards (QS172)
- **QS1:** Women aged 16+ presenting with symptoms are asked about endometriosis symptoms
- **QS2:** Women referred to gynaecology services if symptoms persist despite treatment
- **QS3:** Women with suspected/confirmed deep endometriosis referred to specialist endometriosis centre

#### D. NHS England Severe Endometriosis Service Specification
- Defines criteria for **BSGE-accredited centres** (minimum surgical volumes, MDT requirements)
- Specialist centres must perform laparoscopic excision of all endometriosis including bowel/bladder involvement
- Discharge to secondary/primary care after 6 months follow-up
- **URL:** https://www.england.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2018/08/Complex-gynaecology-severe-endometriosis.pdf

#### E. NCEPOD 2024 Report: "A Long and Painful Road"
- Highlights diagnostic delays (average 8–10 years)
- Recommends awareness raising, structured pathways, and improved referral processes
- **URL:** https://www.ncepod.org.uk/2024endometriosis/

## 3. UK-APPROVED MEDICATIONS FOR ENDOMETRIOSIS

Based on BNF, NICE TAs, and local formulary documents, here is the **complete hierarchy of medications approved in the UK**:

### 🔴 FIRST-LINE: Analgesia (Pain Management)

| Medication | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Paracetamol** | Simple analgesia, does not suppress disease |
| **NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, naproxen, mefenamic acid) | First-line for pain; does not suppress disease |
| **Codeine/Co-codamol** | Step 2 analgesia |
| **Amitriptyline** (low dose) | Pain modifier for chronic pelvic pain (NICE-recommended for neuropathic pain component) |
| **Gabapentin/Pregabalin** | Pain modifier when amitriptyline fails; consider chronic pain clinic referral |

### 🟡 FIRST-LINE HORMONAL: Disease-Suppressing Treatment (NICE NG73)

NICE recommends: *"Offer hormonal treatment (e.g. the combined oral contraceptive pill or a progestogen)"* for suspected, confirmed, or recurrent endometriosis.

| Medication | Route | UK Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Combined Oral Contraceptive (COC)** | Oral | First-line, widely available | Continuous or cyclical use; licensed for endometriosis pain |
| **Levonorgestrel IUS (Mirena® 52mg)** | Intrauterine | First-line | Reduces dysmenorrhoea; useful post-surgery; highly effective contraceptive; erratic bleeding first 4–6 months |
| **Medroxyprogesterone acetate (Provera)** | Oral | First-line progestogen | Alternative when oestrogen contraindicated |
| **Norethisterone** | Oral | First-line progestogen | Commonly used; testosterone derivative side effects possible |
| **Progestogen-only pill (POP)** | Oral | First-line when oestrogen contraindicated | Irregular bleeding common |

### 🟠 SECOND-LINE HORMONAL: GnRH Analogues

| Medication | Route | UK Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Triptorelin (Gonapeptyl)** | Injection (depot) | Licensed, second-line | Requires add-back therapy (tibolone or low-dose estradiol+norethisterone) for >6 months use |
| **Goserelin (Zoladex)** | Subcutaneous implant | Licensed, second-line | Same add-back therapy requirement |
| **Leuprorelin acetate (Prostap)** | Injection | Licensed, second-line | Used as comparator in NICE TA appraisals |
| **Buserelin** | Intranasal / injection | Licensed | Less commonly used |

### 🔵 THIRD-LINE / NEWLY APPROVED: GnRH Antagonists (Daily Oral Pills)

These are the **newest additions** to the UK endometriosis toolkit — both NICE-approved in 2025:

| Medication | Brand | NICE TA | Criteria | Dose | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Relugolix–estradiol–norethisterone** | **Ryeqo®** | **TA1057** (April 2025) | Adults of reproductive age who have **had previous medical or surgical treatment** for endometriosis | 1 tablet daily | First long-term daily pill; can be stopped/started easily for fertility planning; home administration; DXA scan if osteoporosis risk; AMBER classification — specialist initiation |
| **Linzagolix + hormonal add-back therapy** | **Yselty®** | **TA1067** (June 2025) | Adults of reproductive age who have **had previous medical or surgical treatment** for endometriosis | 1 tablet daily (linzagolix + estradiol 1mg + norethisterone acetate 0.5mg add-back) | Second daily oral GnRH antagonist; specialist supervision required; DXA scan recommended if risk factors; both 100mg and 200mg doses available |

### 🟣 SPECIALIST-ONLY: Dienogest (Third-Line Progestogen)

| Medication | Brand(s) | UK Status | Criteria | Dose |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Dienogest** | **Visanne®/Zalkya®/Dimetrum®/Sawis®** / generics | BNF-listed, **AMBER 0** (specialist initiation) | When COCs and progestogen-only contraceptives **have proved ineffective or not tolerated** | 2mg once daily |

- Listed in BNF: https://bnf.nice.org.uk/drugs/dienogest
- Local formularies class as **third-line** (after COC and POP/progestogens failed)
- Comparable efficacy to GnRH agonists per systematic review
- More expensive than other progestogens and COCs
